✨ BMR — Basal Metabolic Rate
The number of calories your body burns at complete rest — just to keep your heart beating, lungs breathing, and organs functioning. Think of it as your body's "idle" energy consumption.
Male: (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) − (5 × age) + 5
Female: (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) − (5 × age) − 161
🔥 TDEE — Total Daily Energy Expenditure
The total calories you burn per day when your activity level is factored in. This is your true maintenance calorie number — eating at this level keeps your weight stable.
TDEE = BMR × Activity Multiplier
📏 BMI — Body Mass Index
A simple screening tool that estimates whether your weight is in a healthy range for your height. Note: BMI does not measure body fat directly and doesn't account for muscle mass, age, or bone density.
BMI = weight(kg) ÷ height(m)²
<18.5 Underweight | 18.5–24.9 Normal | 25–29.9 Overweight | 30–34.9 Obese I | 35–39.9 Obese II | ≥40 Obese III
⚖️ DBW — Desirable Body Weight (Hamwi Formula)
The recommended body weight for your height based on the Hamwi formula, commonly used in clinical nutrition settings to calculate nutritional requirements. Note: this formula is validated for adults of average stature and extrapolates below its range for heights under 5ft.
Male: 112 + (4 × inches above 5ft) → ÷ 2.2046 = kg
Female: 106 + (4 × inches above 5ft) → ÷ 2.2046 = kg
🔄 AdjBW — Adjusted Body Weight
Applied automatically when BMI ≥ 25 (overweight or obese). AdjBW corrects for the fact that excess body fat is less metabolically active than lean mass, giving a more appropriate weight for nutrition calculations. Shown as N/A when not applicable.
AdjBW = DBW + 0.25 × (Actual Weight − DBW)
🥗 Macronutrients (Protein, Fat, Carbs)
Protein targets are tiered by activity level (1.0–2.0 g/kg). Fat is set at 0.8 g/kg. Carbohydrates fill the remaining calories after protein and fat. The weight used for macros is automatically adjusted based on BMI (AdjBW if overweight, DBW if underweight, actual weight if normal). Macros shown are based on maintenance calories — adjust proportionally for fat loss or muscle gain goals.
Protein = weight × 1.0–2.0 g/kg (by activity)
Fat = weight × 0.8 g/kg
Carbs = remaining calories ÷ 4

Choosing the right level is important — selecting too high will overestimate your calorie needs. When unsure, err on the lower side.

🔥 Calorie Needs

Four numbers are shown. Each tells you how many calories to eat per day depending on your goal.

🟢 Maintenance
Eat this to keep your current weight. This equals your TDEE — the total calories you burn each day including activity.
💡 Example: If your result is 2,100 cal/day — eating exactly 2,100 calories means your weight stays the same.
✨ BMR
The minimum calories your body needs to survive at rest. Never eat below this number — it represents your organs, heartbeat, and breathing energy.
💡 Example: BMR of 1,550 cal — even on rest days, your body burns at least this much just existing.
🔻 Fat Loss
Eat this to lose about 0.5 kg per week. This is Maintenance minus 500 calories. A daily 500-calorie deficit = 3,500 cal/week = roughly 0.45 kg of fat.
💡 Example: Maintenance is 2,100 → Fat Loss target is 1,600 cal/day. Eating 1,600 cal daily = lose ≈ 2 kg/month.
🔺 Muscle Gain
Eat this to build muscle while minimising fat gain. This is Maintenance plus 300 calories — a modest surplus that fuels muscle growth without excessive fat storage.
💡 Example: Maintenance is 2,100 → Muscle Gain target is 2,400 cal/day. Pair this with resistance training.

📊 Body Metrics

These values describe your body composition and are used to personalise your nutrition targets.

📏 BMI — Body Mass Index
A quick screening number based on your height and weight. It tells you whether your weight falls in a healthy range. Note: BMI does not distinguish fat from muscle — a very muscular person may show a high BMI but still be healthy.
Underweight <18.5
May need to increase calorie intake. See a doctor.
Normal 18.5–24.9
Healthy range. Focus on maintenance and fitness.
Overweight 25–29.9
AdjBW is used for nutrition calculations.
Obese Class I 30–34.9
Increased health risk. Medical guidance advised.
Obese Class II 35–39.9
High health risk. Consult a healthcare professional.
Obese Class III ≥40
Very high risk. Prompt medical evaluation strongly recommended.
⚖️ DBW — Desirable Body Weight
The ideal healthy weight for your height, calculated using the Hamwi formula. This is the reference target your nutrition numbers are anchored to.
💡 Example: A 5'7" (170 cm) male → DBW ≈ 65 kg. This is the benchmark for his ideal weight.
🔄 AdjBW — Adjusted Body Weight
Used when BMI ≥ 25. Since excess body fat burns fewer calories than lean tissue, AdjBW gives a more accurate weight to base nutrition on. It's calculated as DBW plus 25% of the excess weight.
💡 Example: Actual weight 90 kg, DBW 65 kg → AdjBW = 65 + (0.25 × 25) = 71.25 kg. Macros are based on 71.25 kg, not 90 kg.
🎯 Weight to Lose
Shown when BMI ≥ 25. This is the difference between your actual body weight and your Adjusted Body Weight (AdjBW). It gives you a practical target to work towards, not the absolute minimum, but a clinically meaningful reduction.
Weight to Lose = Actual Body Weight − AdjBW
💡 Example: Actual weight 90 kg, AdjBW 71.25 kg → Weight to Lose = 18.75 kg. This is your practical reduction goal.

🥗 Daily Macronutrient Targets

Macros are the three main nutrients your body needs every day. Use the "Show macros for" dropdown above the macro tiles to switch between Maintenance, Fat Loss, and Muscle Gain targets. The protein and fat amounts stay the same — only carbohydrates change to match your calorie goal.

🟢 Maintenance
Carbs calculated from TDEE calories. Protein & fat same across all goals.
🔻 Fat Loss
Carbs reduced to match TDEE − 500 cal. Lower carbs = lower total calories.
🔺 Muscle Gain
Carbs increased to match TDEE + 300 cal. More carbs = more fuel for training.
🥩 Protein
Builds and repairs muscle. Keeps you full. Essential after any kind of exercise. Target: 1.0–2.0 g per kg of body weight depending on activity level. Protein target is the same regardless of goal — your muscles need the same support whether you're cutting or bulking.
💡 Example: 70 kg moderately active person → target ≈ 112 g protein/day (1.6 g/kg). That's roughly 4 chicken breasts.
🥑 Fat
Supports hormone production, vitamin absorption (A, D, E, K), and brain health. Target: 0.8 g per kg of body weight. Fat target is the same regardless of goal — keeping fat consistent protects hormone balance during calorie changes.
💡 Example: 70 kg person → target ≈ 56 g fat/day. That's about 4 tablespoons of olive oil worth of fat.
🍚 Carbohydrates
Your body's primary energy source, especially during exercise. Carbs are the only macro that changes between goals — they fill the remaining calories after protein and fat are set. Lower for fat loss, higher for muscle gain.
💡 Example (same 70 kg person, 2,100 cal maintenance):
Maintenance → 287 g carbs | Fat Loss → 162 g carbs | Muscle Gain → 362 g carbs

The NDAP Exchange System is a diet planning method used by registered dietitians and nutritionists in the Philippines. Developed under the Nutritional Guidelines by the National Nutrition Council, it organises foods into groups based on similar calorie content and nutrients. One standard portion within a group is called an exchange, and foods within the same group can substitute each other without changing total calorie intake.

🍚 Rice / Starch — 80 kcal per exchange
The primary carbohydrate source. Forms the foundation of the Filipino diet.
½ cup cooked rice = 1 slice bread = ½ cup pasta = 1 small potato → 1 Exchange
🍌 Fruit — 60 kcal per exchange
Natural sugars and vitamins. Usually distributed to breakfast and snacks.
1 small banana = 1 medium apple = ½ cup mango = 1 cup papaya → 1 Exchange
🥦 Vegetables — 25 kcal per exchange
Low calorie, high fibre. Distributed mainly in lunch and dinner meals.
½ cup carrots = 1 cup cabbage = ½ cup green beans → 1 Exchange
🥩 Meat / Protein — 55 kcal per exchange
Lean protein sources. Each exchange provides approximately 7g of protein.
30g chicken = 30g fish = 1 egg = ½ cup tofu → 1 Exchange
🥛 Milk — 120 kcal per exchange
Calcium-rich group. Optional and typically included in snacks.
1 cup fresh milk = 1 cup yogurt → 1 Exchange
🫒 Fat — 45 kcal per exchange
Concentrated energy source. Use sparingly for cooking and dressings.
1 tsp cooking oil = 1 tsp butter = 1 tsp mayonnaise → 1 Exchange

🧮 How Exchanges Are Calculated

The calculator converts your daily calorie goal (based on TDEE and your selected goal) into food exchanges using NDAP's standard calorie distribution:

Rice Exchanges  = (Calories × 0.40) ÷ 80
Meat Exchanges  = (Calories × 0.20) ÷ 55
Fat Exchanges   = (Calories × 0.20) ÷ 45
Fruit Exchanges  = (Calories × 0.10) ÷ 60
Veg Exchanges   = (Calories × 0.10) ÷ 25
💡 Example: 2,000 kcal/day
🍚 Rice: (2000 × 0.40) ÷ 80 = 10 exchanges
🥩 Meat: (2000 × 0.20) ÷ 55 = 7 exchanges
🫒 Fat: (2000 × 0.20) ÷ 45 = 9 exchanges
🍌 Fruit: (2000 × 0.10) ÷ 60 = 3 exchanges
🥦 Veg: (2000 × 0.10) ÷ 25 = 8 exchanges
